Dr. Heather Leffew is an executive data science leader and behavioral systems modeler with over twenty years of experience in AI strategy, digital transformation, and quantitative research. With a PhD in Psychology focusing on quantitative predictive linguistics, her career has been defined by a single objective: building mathematically rigorous, scalable systems capable of mapping complex human behavior.

Her trajectory from clinical neurotherapy into enterprise data architecture is uncommon but entirely deliberate. Early in her career, she conducted forensic and diagnostic psychiatric evaluations, responding to critical incidents and modeling the neurobiology of severe trauma. Her doctoral research investigated the linguistics of mass violence. She utilized computer-mediated psychometrics to map how implicit power drives within written manifestos reliably escalate into physical threat trajectories. That clinical foundation forced a realization: technology is not neutral; it is a behavioral intervention executed at scale. To address systemic harm, Dr. Leffew needed to master the engineering pipelines that dictate those interventions across massive populations.

At TikTok, as a Principal Data Scientist and Applied Researcher, Dr. Leffew took ownership of the measurement and detection capabilities protecting platform integrity. She developed analytics suites processing over one billion records and architected the statistical safety frameworks necessary to detect semantic coercion and algorithmic harm. Her team mapped cross-modal escalations, deployed automated detection systems at 95% accuracy, and established an industry-first external IRB methodology prioritizing ethical review of global research.

As Director of Data Science at Spokeo, Dr. Leffew built and scaled the AI function to completely transform how identity is codified. She led the development of a proprietary 15-step distributed machine learning pipeline powering a 49-terabyte knowledge graph. This infrastructure replaced subjective record linkage with a systematic entity resolution engine relying on graph machine learning and weak supervision. By defining strict accountability and ablation frameworks, her organization reduced core processing time from 150 hours to under 36 hours. They deployed NLP-driven semantic clustering engines capable of organizing thousands of raw vendor schemas into a strict canonical taxonomy, fundamentally shifting the corporate data procurement strategy.

Before focusing exclusively on massive consumer technology, Dr. Leffew directed evaluations, analytics, and accreditation for national public safety organizations. She led digital transformations that migrated legacy psychological assessments into scalable cloud architectures, improving operational throughput five-fold across a thousand client agencies. Applying rigorous evidence-based measurement, she developed predictive analytics for mental health service forecasting, securing massive grant funding for behavioral science innovation and establishing stringent compliance protocols alongside national regulatory bodies.

Today, her technical stack relies heavily on PySpark, AWS SageMaker, graph ML, and deep reinforcement learning. The absolute tooling is always secondary to the architectural requirement that models must generate interpretable, fairness-aware outcomes under intense ambiguity. Whether she is deploying late-fusion multimodal systems for clinical assessment or architecting predictive classification algorithms that decouple statistical probability from raw contract magnitude, the standard remains absolute. Dr. Leffew builds scalable intelligence infrastructures that embed safety, psychometric validity, and ethical boundaries directly into production environments. Having psychologists design the systems that govern human beings makes perfect sense.
