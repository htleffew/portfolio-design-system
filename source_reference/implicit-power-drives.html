Implicit Power Drives in the Manifestos Preceding Autogenic Massacres

Heather T. Leffew
scroll to begin
Context
The Phenomenon of Autogenic Massacres

Power drive language elevated above all tested comparison baselines, including the multi-source composite. That breadth of elevation, consistent across comparison groups, suggests the result is a property of the corpus as a whole. In practical terms, this consistency was the directional finding that proved we could track and quantify dangerous mindsets mathematically, ultimately motivating the larger, conclusive typological study in the final dissertation.

Methodology
Linguistic Corpus & Statistical Framework

This study executed a computational psycholinguistic analysis quantifying power drive language across n=23 autogenic massacres. The linguistic corpus generated 111,811 unredacted, author-generated words covering the final manifestos and digital artifacts of all qualifying subjects.

The statistical framework leveraged LIWC2015 to extract normalized frequencies of power-oriented lexicography. By setting conservative α=0.01 parameters across Independent Samples T-Tests, we established a mathematically rigorous boundary separating autogenic perpetrators from matched generalized populations.

Results
Quantifying the Action Threshold

A series of Independent Samples T-Tests assuming unequal variances (Welch’s t-test) revealed highly significant absolute differences. The mean frequency of power words expressed by autogenic perpetrators (M=3.00, SD=1.28) was consistently and severely elevated above every comparison baseline.

Linguistics
Semantic Cartography of the Action Mindset

Examining the specific semantic tokens driving these statistical elevations reveals a profound cognitive narrowing. The language shifts aggressively away from self-reflection toward externalized domination, out-group alienation, and rigid moral imperatives that serve to cognitively bypass natural inhibitions against violence.

Findings
Practical Application & Future Horizons
Conclusions
  • ▸ Predictive Efficacy: Elevated "power drive" frequency functions as a reliable linguistic telemetry point differentiating lethal autogenic perpetrators from the general population.
  • ▸ Targeted Modeling: The consistency across diverse comparative baseline models establishes robustness—validating implicit motive markers as viable features for predictive text classifiers.
  • ▸ Psychological Shift: The language indicates a severe operational transition—away from self-oriented depression towards externalized grievance and the perceived operational requirement for violence.
Next Phase: Warning Behaviors
This research directly cascaded into the core behavioral models governing our predictive AI validation systems. By identifying that linguistic markers of 'power' are measurable precursors to catastrophic action, we laid the groundwork for integrating NLP telemetry engines into live clinical and security environments, shifting from reactive assessment to proactive intervention.
