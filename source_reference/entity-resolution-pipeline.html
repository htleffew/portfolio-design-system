Resolution Pipeline

Mapping the Resolution Layer

An entity resolution layer determines which phone number, address, court record, or social profile belongs to which person. Every downstream product that consumes identity data depends on it, and quality failures in the layer propagate directly to the product.

The legacy pipeline processed identity fusion through a series of deterministic heuristic rules. Because the full logic structure of these heuristics was not formally documented within internal repositories, the system was historically constrained to a 60-day batch execution cadence. Furthermore, based on indirect observations, an internal assumption had developed that one in eleven person identifiers shifted structurally with every iteration, presenting a significant stability risk.

The objective of this research program was to establish rigorous empirical measurement over the pipeline, mapping its architecture constraints and definitively calculating the actual variance rate across iterations.

Structural Forensics

The 17-Rule SSN Waterfall

Forensic analysis of the v13 pipeline checkpoint revealed the full match architecture for the first time. Seventeen rules operate in a strict waterfall sequence, processing 75 gigabytes of edge data before producing a single person identifier. They divide into four tiers: eight SSN-anchored rules, three DOB-plus-phone rules, three DOB-plus-email rules, and three DOB-plus-address rules.

At 49.5 gigabytes of edge volume, it constitutes the graph's structural backbone. Any architectural change displacing SSN-based linking without a validated replacement would dissolve two-thirds of the graph's identity associations.

Longitudinal Analysis

Longitudinal Stability Measurement

To formalize the actual identifier churn rate, the team developed a deterministic 1% hash-aligned sampling protocol: rather than random sampling, which would have fractured cluster integrity by dropping neighbors, the hash-alignment ensured that every record associated with a sampled person was retrieved identically across all 59 data sources.

Analysis covered 96 million records across pipeline versions v12 and v13. The aggregate PID retention rate measured across these versions was 99.64%. Of the 0.36% that did register as changed, 98.2% were cosmetic re-labeling artifacts where the cluster integrity and adjacent neighbors remained identical. Substantive churn, defined as records genuinely moving between distinct people, measured at 0.004%.

Defect Isolation

Partial-Null Bridging

With substantive churn measured at 0.004%, the research turned to actual quality failures. Cluster face-validity review isolated a specific defect pattern in the Court Records tier (source 33_01_01): Partial-Null Bridging.

The mechanism is a three-record transitive closure failure. Person A holds SSN_1. Person B holds SSN_2. A third record, Record C, carries a null SSN but matches both Person A and Person B on name and date of birth. The pipeline's union-find algorithm observes that A equals C and B equals C, and concludes that A equals B. Two distinct people merge into one identity. The defect surface contained 35,097 clusters of this type.

Variant B Repair

For every cluster containing multiple non-null SSNs, the job splits by SSN anchor, extracts each null-SSN row's (DOB, last-name) signature, and reassigns the row to its highest-similarity anchor. Records that cannot be attributed with confidence are discarded. Zero record orphaning.

Model Evaluation

Supervised Model vs. Heuristics

The first ML benchmark framed a single research question: does a supervised classifier earn a role beyond the hardened Variant B repair? A logistic regression trained on six features from the conservative label packet achieved an AUC of 0.9996 on the held-out test set. The heuristic proxy accuracy on the identical packet was also 0.9996. The model had learned to reconstruct the heuristic precisely. It added no discriminating information of its own.

The more telling evaluation was the abstain surface: the 137,698 unlabeled records the heuristic had deliberately withheld from judgment. At a probability threshold of 0.90, 81 records resolved to exactly one high-confidence candidate. The remaining 137,617 were still ambiguous or empty. The model was amplifying the same uncertainty the heuristic had already identified, not resolving it. The formal decision: ml_candidate_recapitulates_heuristic.

Phase 12 Verdict

Bounded Posture Stands

Phase 12 addressed a final question: should the bounded repair and shadow-ML posture be displaced by a wholesale Mixture-of-Experts architecture? The examination defined an 11-gate acceptance bar covering contradiction behavior, PID stability, deployment posture, and ML safety. No alternative architecture cleared all 11 criteria on retained evidence.

The Phase 12 verdict, bounded_posture_stands, formalized the recommendation: Variant B surgical repair for the static batch surface, an incremental resolution path for customer-facing identity, and ML held in shadow mode. Any future proposal to reopen the architecture requires a new validation wave, not a reinterpretation of the retained record.
