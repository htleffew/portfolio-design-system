Entity resolution at Spokeo: measure first, rebuild only if the measurements ask.

A research program against a 49TB consumer-identity graph that began as a rebuild and turned into a measurement. The legacy pipeline ran on a 17-rule heuristic nobody had written down; the operational lore held that one in eleven person identifiers shifted per iteration. The program forensically recovered the rules, measured the actual churn at 0.004%, found the one place the graph really did fail, repaired it surgically, and decided not to rebuild the rest.

Surface The Consumer Identity Graph: 49TB across 59 vendor source partitions, ~8B person-graph rows, 60-day batch cadence on a 17-rule undocumented heuristic
Method Forensic recovery of the rules, deterministic 1% hash-aligned stability sampling across 96M records, defect isolation, surgical repair, and a supervised-model bake-off against the heuristic on its own abstain surface
Outcome One defect class found and repaired (35,097 clusters, zero record orphaning); core processing time reduced from 150+ hours to 24 to 36 hours; ML held in shadow mode after the model recapitulated the heuristic exactly
01 / Setup

Measure first. Rebuild only if the measurements ask.

The Consumer Identity Graph at Spokeo determines which phone number, address, court record, or social profile belongs to which person, and every downstream product that consumes identity data depends on it. The graph stood at roughly 49TB across 59 vendor source partitions, with about eight billion person-graph rows in the live state. It ran on a 60-day batch cadence against a deterministic heuristicThe legacy heuristic, a 17-rule waterfall that decides which records belong to the same person, executed in strict sequence on roughly 75GB of edge data per iteration. Its full logic was not formally documented inside any internal repository at the start of the program. nobody had written down. Internal lore held that one in eleven person identifiers shifted structurally with every iteration, an estimate of about 9.09% churn that nobody had measured but several teams were quietly pricing into roadmap decisions19.09% was 1 in 11. Nobody had measured 1 in 11; somebody had said "about one in ten or eleven" once and the number had hardened into a planning input. Production lore reaches roadmaps the same way every time..

The brief the program started with was the inverse of the obvious one. The obvious move was to replace the heuristic with a learned model and call the resulting reduction in churn a result. The brief instead was to measure the system that already existed: forensically recover the rules, calculate the actual churn rate against retained evidence, isolate where the graph genuinely failed, and only then decide whether to rearchitect anything. The constraint was that any architectural change displacing SSN-based linking, which an unmeasured rebuild was likely to do by accident, would dissolve roughly two-thirds of the graph's identity associations on the way out the door.

What follows is twenty research notebooks, NB01 through NB20, condensed to a case study. The findings are not flattering to the team's first instinct. The folk churn estimate was wrong by three orders of magnitude. The supervised model trained against the heuristic's evidence reproduced the heuristic exactly. The architectural review at Phase 12, against an explicit eleven-gate bar, declined to displace the bounded-repair posture with a Mixture-of-Experts rebuild. Each of those is in this writeup, in plain language, because measuring a system honestly only matters if the conclusions are reported when they are inconvenient.

What the program was for
The brief was not "build a better entity resolver." The brief was "find out what the current entity resolver actually does, find out what it actually breaks on, and fix only what the evidence proves is broken." The discipline this required, against the team's standing instinct to rebuild, is the only reason the case study exists. A program that had skipped the measurement phase would have shipped a Mixture-of-Experts replacement and reported it as a win against a churn rate it had never measured.
02 / Architecture

The architecture nobody had written down.

Forensic analysis of the v13 pipeline checkpoint recovered the full match architecture for the first time. Seventeen rules execute in strict waterfall sequence, processing roughly 75GB of edge data per iteration before producing a single person identifier. They divide into four tiers by anchor: eight rules anchored on Social Security number, three on date of birth combined with phone, three on date of birth combined with email, and three on date of birth combined with address. The order matters. The first rule that matches a record assigns its person identifier and the remaining rules see the record as already decided.

Fig. 1 . Edge volume by anchor category in the v13 checkpoint. The eight SSN-anchored rules account for 49.5GB of the roughly 75GB of edge data, or 66.93% of all matched edges. The graph's structural backbone is SSN; any architectural change that displaces SSN linking without a validated replacement dissolves two-thirds of the identity associations the graph holds.

The recovered structure is the precondition for everything that follows. Without the rule order written down, the team could not have constructed the conservative label packet against which the supervised model in Sec. 06 was eventually trained, could not have isolated the Partial-Null Bridging defect to a specific tier, and could not have argued at Phase 12 that the bounded posture was defensible. A pipeline you cannot describe is a pipeline you cannot honestly evaluate, and forensic recovery, which is unglamorous and yields no model artifact, was the most consequential single piece of work in the program.

03 / Stability

A folk estimate, off by three orders of magnitude.

To formalize the actual identifier churn rate, the team developed a deterministic 1% hash-alignedHash-aligned sampling, a sampling protocol that selects records by a deterministic hash of a stable key (here the person identifier) rather than by a random draw. Because the hash is deterministic, every record associated with a sampled person is retrieved identically across all sources and pipeline versions, preserving cluster integrity in the sample. sampling protocol. Random sampling would have fractured cluster integrity by dropping the neighbors of every sampled person, which would have inflated the apparent churn and produced a number that measured the sampling artifact rather than the system2Random 1% sampling would have dropped neighbors of every sampled person and inflated the apparent churn. The hash alignment is the difference between measuring stability and measuring sampling artifact.. The hash alignment ensured every record associated with a sampled person was retrieved identically across all 59 data sources and across pipeline versions v12 and v13.

Analysis covered 96 million records. The aggregate person-identifier retention rate measured across versions v12 and v13 was 99.64%. Of the 0.36% that did register as changed, 98.2% were cosmetic re-labeling artifacts in which the cluster integrity and adjacent neighbors remained identical, the same set of people simply receiving a different identifier string. Substantive churn, defined as records genuinely moving between distinct people, measured at 0.004%. The folk estimate of 9.09% was off by roughly three orders of magnitude.

Fig. 2 . The folk estimate of 9.09% identifier churn (left) against the measured substantive churn of 0.004% (right). Both panels use the same vertical scale; the measured value is a sliver against the assumed value. The pipeline was being managed against a number that was wrong by a factor of roughly two thousand.
How the 1% sample was constructed
Deterministic, not random. Each candidate record was hashed on a stable key, and a fixed hash range determined membership in the sample; the same record falls in or out of the sample identically every time. The consequence is that every neighbor of a sampled person is also retrieved, so the sample is a 1% slice of intact clusters rather than a 1% scatter of orphaned rows. Random 1% sampling, applied to the same task, would have produced a churn estimate dominated by the sampling itself, not by the pipeline. A measurement protocol that does not survive scrutiny does not produce numbers worth managing against.
04 / Defect

The one place the graph really did fail.

With substantive churn measured at 0.004%, the program turned to actual quality failures. Cluster face-validity review across the conservative label packet isolated a single defect pattern in the Court Records tier, vendor source 33_01_01, that the team named Partial-Null Bridging. The mechanism is a three-record transitive closure failure that the pipeline's union-findUnion-find, a classical algorithm for maintaining disjoint sets under merge operations. Given a sequence of "A and B belong to the same group" assertions, it efficiently answers whether X and Y belong to the same group. Its transitive closure is a feature of the algorithm by design; it also makes the algorithm uncritical of the assertions it receives. aggregator could not see and was structurally unable to reject. Person A holds SSN_1. Person B holds SSN_2. A third record, Record C, carries a null SSN but matches both Person A and Person B on date of birth and last name. The aggregator observes that A equals C and B equals C, and concludes by transitive closure that A equals B. Two distinct people merge into one identity. The defect surface contained 35,097 clusters of this type.

Fig. 3 . The Partial-Null Bridging mechanism (left) and the Variant B repair (right). For every cluster containing multiple non-null SSN anchors, the repair job splits by SSN anchor, extracts each null-SSN row's date-of-birth and last-name signature, and reassigns the row to its highest-similarity anchor. Records that cannot be attributed with confidence are not orphaned, they are routed back into the conservative label packet for downstream review.

Two facts about the defect bound everything that follows. First, it is local: the failure is in the aggregator's transitive closure on a particular shape of evidence, not in the rules themselves, and not in the input data. Second, it is bounded: the defect surface is enumerable from the v13 checkpoint, and it is finite. Both facts are what made a surgical repair viable, and both facts are what made a wholesale rearchitecture, which would have rebuilt the parts of the pipeline that were not broken in order to reach the part that was, the wrong tool.

05 / Repair

Variant B, surgical, zero record orphaning.

The repair job, named Variant B in the program record, runs against the enumerated defect surface. For every cluster containing two or more non-null SSN anchors, the job splits the cluster by SSN, extracts each null-SSN row's (date of birth, last name) signature, and reassigns the row to its highest-similarity SSN anchor. Records that fall below the similarity threshold are not orphaned; they are routed back into the conservative label packet that the supervised model in Sec. 06 was eventually trained against. The bar the repair was held to was zero record orphaning, because orphaning a record silently reintroduces the same class of failure on the next iteration through a different shape of evidence.

The alternative the program rejected was a learned tie-break: train a model on the same (DOB, last-name, candidate anchor) feature set and let it choose the anchor. The rejection was not on principle. The rejection was that the candidate evidence, when written down honestly, is identical to what the heuristic split-by-anchor procedure already used, and that the model would have introduced calibration, drift, and shadow-mode operational requirements without producing a different decision on the conservative cases or a defensible decision on the ambiguous ones. The repair did the available work, and it did it deterministically.

The repair is the smallest piece of the program by line count and the part the team is most willing to defend in writing. Smallness is the property: Variant B is auditable, it is reversible against a held checkpoint, and it touches only the cluster shapes the v13 forensic recovery proved were broken. A repair that is small and bounded is a repair that can be understood by an on-call engineer at three in the morning, and that is the bar production identity infrastructure has to meet, not the bar of architectural elegance.

06 / ML benchmark

The model learned to be the heuristic.

With the repair shipped against the bounded defect, the program turned to the question the team had wanted to ask first: whether a supervised classifier earned a role beyond Variant B. A logistic regression trained on six features from the conservative label packet achieved an AUC of 0.9996 on the held-out test set. The heuristic proxy accuracy on the identical packet was also 0.9996. The model had learned to reconstruct the heuristic precisely; it added no discriminating information of its own.

The more telling evaluation was against the heuristic's abstain surfaceAbstain surface, the set of cases the heuristic deliberately did not decide on, either because the evidence was contradictory or because no rule produced a confident match. It is the fairest test set for a model: cases where the existing system has already declared "I do not know," and where any new information the model introduces would represent genuine value.: the 137,698 unlabeled records the heuristic had deliberately withheld from judgment. At a probability threshold of 0.90, 81 records resolved to exactly one high-confidence candidate. The remaining 137,617 were still ambiguous or empty. The model was amplifying the same uncertainty the heuristic had already identified, not resolving it. The formal decision recorded against this evaluation was ml_candidate_recapitulates_heuristic.

Fig. 4 . The abstain-surface bake-off. Of 137,698 cases the heuristic deliberately withheld from judgment, the supervised model resolved 81 at a probability threshold of 0.90. AUC on the labeled portion was identical to the heuristic at 0.9996, and the ML candidate was formally recorded as recapitulating the heuristic.
What ml_recapitulates_heuristic means, and what it does not
The model is not bad. The data is not bad. The training procedure is not bad. The decision surface the heuristic produces is the decision surface the available evidence supports, and a model trained on the same evidence retrieves the same surface. Recapitulates is the word the program record uses because fails would be wrong. The right operational consequence is to hold ML in shadow mode, where it observes production decisions and accumulates labeled disagreement cases, against a future cohort of evidence the heuristic was not trained against. Promoting the model into the decision path on this evidence would be production theater.
07 / Verdict

The bounded posture stands.

Phase 12 addressed the question the program had been built to answer honestly: whether the bounded-repair posture should be displaced by a wholesale rearchitecture.

The architectural review defined an eleven-gate acceptance bar covering contradiction behavior under conflicting evidence, person-identifier stability across versions, deployment posture (shadow, canary, general availability), and ML safety (calibration, abstention, drift). A Mixture-of-ExpertsMixture of Experts (MoE), an architecture pattern in which a gating network routes inputs to one of several specialized expert models. In identity resolution it would mean a router deciding, per record, which of several learned linkage strategies to apply. rebuild proposal cleared eight of the eleven; no alternative architecture cleared all eleven against retained evidence3The gates aren't trivia: contradiction behavior under conflicting evidence, PID stability across versions, deployment posture (shadow / canary / general), and ML safety (calibration, abstention, drift). A Mixture-of-Experts proposal cleared eight.. The verdict recorded against Phase 12 was bounded_posture_stands: Variant B surgical repair for the static batch surface, an incremental resolution path for customer-facing identity, and ML held in shadow mode. Any future proposal to reopen the architecture requires a new validation wave against fresh evidence, not a reinterpretation of the retained record.

The operational outcome the program produced was concrete. Core processing time fell from more than 150 hours per iteration to 24 to 36 hours, against a 17-rule architecture that had been left structurally intact. Eighty engineers across eight cross-functional squads consumed identity from the graph throughout, and the v14 checkpoint shipped on cadence with the conservative label packet, the Variant B repair, and the shadow-mode ML observer running in parallel. The most valuable artifact the program produced was a number, 0.004%, and a willingness to let that number determine whether the rest of the proposal still made sense.

What was learned
Three things, all of them uncomfortable in different ways. The first is that production lore is data, and the data was wrong by three orders of magnitude. The second is that the team's instinct to rebuild was not specific to this team; it is the default instinct of anyone trained on machine-learning systems, and the brief that produced the Phase 12 verdict was not "be skeptical of ML," it was "measure first." The third is that the strongest finding of the program, that nothing major needed to be replaced, is the kind of finding that disappears from a roadmap unless it is written down. The case study you are reading is the written record.
