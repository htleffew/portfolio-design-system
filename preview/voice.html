Prose System / VOICE.md

One voice descriptor for the portfolio.

Editorial, methodological, research paper. The system is the subject; the author appears when honesty requires it.

Operative rules

Punctuation, person, sentence weight.

No em dashes or en dashes inside sentences. Pauses use commas, semicolons, colons, and parentheticals. Hyphens carry compound modifiers.

The system, methodology, problem, or approach is the grammatical subject of most sentences. First-person I is permitted where the author is genuinely the agent of an action. we is reserved for the field. you is almost never used.

Long, clause-rich sentences are correct when the underlying material is connected; the cure for clipped, staccato prose is richer punctuation and subordinate structure, not shorter sentences. A sentence does not begin with a bare This, That, These, Those, or Which standing alone as the subject (sentence-initial It is excluded).

Functional and mechanical analogies are permitted. Poetic analogies are banned. Condescending over-simplification is banned.

Worked specimen

From clinical-validation.html.

Canonical Aggregation is two-step. Within a domain, the five families are averaged with soft voting (mean of class probabilities). Across the three domains, the calibrated submodel outputs are averaged with equal weights to produce the final risk score. There is no neural meta-learner stitching the streams together, just a weighted mean.
Wrong Aggregation is a sophisticated two-step process that elegantly combines the strengths of multiple model families, leveraging powerful ensembles to deliver a seamless, robust solution.

The canonical version opens every sentence with the system as subject. The wrong version describes its own attributes and pulls in five banned terms.

II.Grammatical subject, system over author
III.Vocabulary, negative glossary search
IV.Structural rhetoric, no bare demonstratives, no clipped pairs

VI.Genre, one of three, committed at front matter
VII.Quantification, units and stakes inside
VIII.Closing, declarative and quiet
