## Section 2: Three-Mechanism Model (Source lines 13-22)

The playlist's effectiveness is explained through three simultaneous mechanisms:
1. Bilateral stimulation via left-right audio panning (EMDR-adjacent grounding)
2. High tempo (140-150 BPM) providing optimal arousal for under-stimulated ADHD brains
3. Polyrhythmic complexity preventing habituation

**Key detail to preserve:** These are not three separate effects; they are a coordinated system. The source explicitly states the combined effect creates "high-stimulation, low-anxiety" which is the precise state ADHD brains need for sustained deep work. Each mechanism addresses a different failure mode: under-arousal, anxiety/restlessness, and habituation.

## Section 3: Theoretical Grounding (Source lines 24-43)

Each mechanism maps to established theory:
- BPM maps to Optimal Stimulation Theory (Zentall): ADHD brains are chronically under-aroused and seek extra-task stimulation
- Bilateral panning maps to Bilateral Stimulation (BLS) from EMDR therapy: regulates parasympathetic nervous system, reduces fight-or-flight anxiety
- Polyrhythms map to Stochastic Resonance: adding structured noise/complexity enhances signal detection rather than degrading it

**Key detail to preserve:** The ADHD community's adoption of "8D Audio" (exaggerated panning) is mentioned as a community-driven empirical finding. The mechanism is spatial: if the spatial awareness system is busy tracking sound movement, it cannot scan the environment for distractions. This is a resource-competition model of attention, not a relaxation model.

**Key detail to preserve:** The Stochastic Resonance framing is important because it is counterintuitive. Adding complexity should theoretically make focus harder, but the principle shows that a specific level of structured noise actually enhances the ability to detect the primary signal (the work). Simple beats fail because they are too predictable; the ADHD brain habituates and seeks novelty elsewhere.

## Section 4: Combined Effect Model (Source lines 45-47)

The three mechanisms form a coherent system:
- BPM: fixes under-arousal (addresses boredom)
- Panning: fixes anxiety (addresses restlessness/paralysis through grounding)
- Polyrhythms: prevents habituation (addresses tuning-out)

**Key detail to preserve:** This is a three-variable system where each variable addresses a distinct ADHD failure mode. The playlist does not work because of any one feature; it works because all three operate simultaneously to create conditions where the ADHD brain's "extra bandwidth" is fully occupied by the auditory stimulus, preventing that bandwidth from seeking distraction.
